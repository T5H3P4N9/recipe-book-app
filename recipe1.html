Chakalaka,Pap and Steak

45-60 MIN

Nutritions

Protein
~50 g
Fat
~35 g
Carbs
~95 g
Calories
~850 kcal

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 2 cups maize meal (pap)
  • 8 cups water (for pap)
  • 4 steaks (150–200 g each)
  • 1–2 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 2 bell peppers, diced
  • 400 g canned tomatoes (or 3 fresh)
  • 1 tbsp curry powder, 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 can baked beans (optional)
  • Salt, pepper, vinegar/lemon to taste
  • Fresh herbs to garnish

Quick steps

  1. Cook pap: whisk maize meal into boiling water and simmer until thick (20–30 min).
  2. Make chakalaka: sauté onion, garlic, carrot and pepper; add tomato, curry and simmer (10–15 min).
  3. Season steaks and cook on a hot pan/grill 3–6 min per side.
  4. Rest steaks 5–10 min; slice.
  5. Serve steak with pap and a spoonful of chakalaka; garnish.

Recipe — Chakalaka, Pap & Steak

1 — Make the pap

Bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Gradually whisk in 1 cup of maize meal (pap), then reduce heat and simmer, stirring often, until thick and smooth (20–30 minutes).

2 — Adjust consistency

If pap is too stiff, stir in a little hot water to reach your preferred texture. Keep covered and warm while you prepare the rest.

3 — Prepare chakalaka base

Heat 1–2 tbsp oil in a pan. Sauté chopped onion and garlic until soft (3–5 minutes). Add grated carrot and diced bell pepper; cook 4–6 minutes.

4 — Finish chakalaka

Add chopped tomato (or canned), curry powder, paprika, and a spoonful of baked beans or sugar if using. Simmer 10–15 minutes and season with salt, pepper and a splash of vinegar to taste.

5 — Prepare steaks

Bring steaks to room temperature, pat dry and season with salt and pepper (add garlic or spices if desired).

6 — Cook steaks

Heat a heavy pan or grill until very hot. Cook steaks 3–6 minutes per side for medium-rare (times vary with thickness). Use a thermometer: 52–57°C for medium-rare.

7 — Rest

Rest steaks 5–10 minutes before slicing to keep them juicy.

8 — Serve

Serve slices of steak alongside pap and a generous spoonful of chakalaka. Garnish with fresh herbs and lemon if you like.
